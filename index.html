Intro / Background





  • Assessment of mindfulness and its importance is raised. Validity of the assessment of mindfulness from various traditional teachings based on research studies in last 10 years has provided different opinions. There is no single behavioral measure of mindfulness. The implementation of many value added components with mindfulness for problems and clinical disorders and how it purports to measure experiences remains unclear. Based on lengthy study, training and practice of mindfulness and meditation from traditional Buddhist approaches, It is argued that the traditional purpose for insight and enlightened action is the starting point of assessing mindfulness.


    • Research Question


    How do measures assessing mindfulness map out to biopsychosocial indicators. ? We identify two related questions: Why do mindfulness interventions show selective effects and fail to generalize to all mindfulness students? Second, why do these very different interventions, presumably involving different psychological mechanisms, consistently result in similar selective effects? The challenge for this research is to develop better assessment measures that can capture relevant behaviors, experiences and biopsychosocial indicators while consistent with basic mindfulness fundamentals. This research is comprised of several studies.
